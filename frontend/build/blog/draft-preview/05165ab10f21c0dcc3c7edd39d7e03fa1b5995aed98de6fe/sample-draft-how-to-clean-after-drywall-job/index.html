How to clean up a drywall job without killing your shop vac

By Muk Buddy· June 15, 2026

Drywall dust is the silent vac-killer on every remodel job. It's a fine powder — much finer than sawdust — and it slips right past a stock paper filter. Once it gets to the motor, it sticks to the brushes and bearings. You'll smell it before you notice the suction loss: a sweet, slightly burnt smell that means your motor windings are running hot.

The 6-minute cleanup that saves the motor

  1. Pre-soak the room. Light mist on every surface with a pump sprayer. Damp dust doesn't fly.
  2. Sweep the lumps first. Drop the chunks into a contractor bag. Vacs are for fines, not fistfuls.
  3. Switch to Muk Buddy. Two-chamber design traps the fines in the outer chamber so they never reach your filter.
  4. HEPA-pass the air. Run a portable HEPA box for 20 minutes after you leave the room. Catches what settled while you were working.
  5. Wipe surfaces wet. A damp microfiber picks up what the vac left behind.
  6. Empty Muk Buddy outside. Rinse it. Dry it. Reload. You're done.

Why a stock filter dies in two weekends

A standard pleated filter has about 0.7 sq ft of media. Drywall dust forms a cake on the filter surface — and the cake compresses with every vacuum cycle. By weekend two, you've lost 60% of suction and you're cooking the motor every time you flip it on.

Muk Buddy's outer chamber holds the fines before they reach the filter media. The filter stays clean. The motor stays cool. You stop replacing $30 bags every Tuesday.
